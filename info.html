In the early twentieth century, there were about 100,000 tigers in the wild with 9 subspecies. Now, that number is 3,900 and about 5,000 tigers are in captive populations.

Inhumane Treatment

The demand for tiger parts has led to the development of deeply inhumane places such as tiger breeding farms, which actively breed and slaughter tigers for their parts. The farms were created with the hopes of dissuading poachers from hunting wild tigers, but some of these farms have only increased the illegal trade.

Habitat Loss

Tigers are large animals that require a lot of land to roam and hunt. They require tall grasses and vegetation to hide in. Tigers, like all animals, need a reliable water and food supply. When these are taken away, tigers cannot survive.
